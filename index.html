Topic analysis of Venmo feed

The goal of this project was to see if we could draw insights into consumer spending behavior — given a person or group of people, could we identify the types of transactions that they use Venmo for?

By scraping Venmo’s public transaction feed I was able to capture information on all public transactions taking place within the app and perform LDA topic analysis on the transaction descriptions.

Utilizing AWS for facial recognition was a cool add-in for how you could query the database and get transaction insight into a specific user.

Check it out on GitHub

Applying ML to basketball predictions

Each March, millions of basketball fans fill out brackets with hopes of perfectly predicting the results of the NCAA tournament. Instead of relying on pundit opinions this year, I tried to leverage historical data and trained a model to make the predictions for me.

With stats from every game since the 2002-03 season, I threw together a random forest utilizing each team’s regular season stats to predict NCAA tournament results.

Due to time constraints, I wasn’t able to make the model as robust as I would have liked (perhaps a 2019 project) and ended up with a bracket that correctly predicted the winner for 30 of the 63 tournament games, good for 34th percentile on ESPN.

How did this measure against the baseline of the bracket I made by hand? Not well—that bracket ended up being in the 86th percentile.

Check it out on GitHub

Can news stories explain stock prices?

Stock prices and news stories are invariably intertwined. Surprisingly, not many tools that exist today combine these two elements.

As part of a 24-hour hackathon at work, my team wanted to see if we could use news stories to provide context to movements in stock price. Was the reason for a company’s big gain last month tied to a favorable earnings report or announcement of a new partnership?

We utilized historical stock price data from TD, News API and IEX’s API to pull news stories and stock prices in real-time, and the Dash Python library to create an interactive dashboard tracking stock prices and overlaying notable news events.

For some companies, this exercise worked great — for JCPenney we were clearly able to attribute a sharp decline in price to a “volatile earnings release”. For other companies such as Amazon, it was more difficult to filter out the signal from the noise and identify the most relevant news stories.

Check it out on GitHub

Predicting customer engagement

Data Clash was a 10 week long, company-wide, Kaggle-style data science competition hosted by my work this fall. The challenge? Predict which customers will call within 30 days about a payment, statement waiver, or other reason.

My team took an ensemble modeling approach, creating separate one-vs-rest classifiers for each of the three classes we hoped to predict. Paired with some creative feature engineering, we were able to produce the second-highest F1 score, and take second place in the competition of over 100 teams (300+ individuals).

We also managed to secure first place in the sub-challenge of predicting the number of days until a customer will call.

Unfortunately I can’t post any of the datasets or code online — as a result of the competition, our work is being leveraged to create a productionalized model.
